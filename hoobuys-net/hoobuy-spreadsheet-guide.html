Reverse-shopping guide: W2C, QC and pre-order checks

W2C means where to cop: where to find an item and how to decide whether it is worth continuing. A useful page should explain category routes, image checks, size notes, warehouse QC and shipping choices instead of only listing cards.

QC is quality control. For shoes, inspect shape, soles, symmetry, size labels and glue marks. For clothing, inspect fabric, stitching, collar, cuffs, print position and care labels. For bags and accessories, inspect hardware, zips, engraving, closures and dimensions.

Public rules for shipping, free photos, extra-photo prices, video, exchange rates and coupons can change. Use the current account, warehouse service panel and checkout, and do not treat an old fixed price as a promise.

A useful W2C flow starts with categories, continues to selected product routes, and finishes with QC, photo or video checks, shipping routes and coupon notes. This is more useful and maintainable than copying product titles.

Pre-order workflow

  1. Choose the product type from a category route.
  2. Open the product route and verify style, colour, size and notes.
  3. Review all QC photos after warehouse arrival, not only the front image.
  4. Request a focused extra photo or video when needed.
  5. Confirm actual weight, volumetric weight, route restrictions, insurance and coupons.
