Reverse-shopping guide: W2C, QC and pre-order checks

W2C means where to cop. The goal is to find a product and decide whether it is worth continuing. A useful route includes categories, image checks, size notes, warehouse QC and shipping choices.

QC means quality control. Check shape and soles for shoes, fabric and stitching for clothing, and hardware, zips and dimensions for accessories.

For shipping, extra photos, video, exchange rates and coupons, use the current account, warehouse service panel and checkout as the final source.

Frequently asked questions

How should a W2C route work?

Use category entrances, product routes and filters to answer where to buy, then provide a checklist for the final decision.

What should I check in QC photos?

Check the whole item first, then details: shoe shape and soles, clothing fabric and stitching, and accessory hardware and dimensions.

How can I tell whether parcel consolidation is worthwhile?

Compare actual weight, volumetric weight and route limits after consolidation. A larger box can raise volumetric weight, so combining parcels is not always cheaper.

When does volumetric weight matter?

Shoe boxes, thick coats, foam packaging and large light items often trigger volumetric weight. Check the estimate before submitting the parcel.

When is a video useful?

Video is useful for moving, opening, lighting, ports, zips or details that are difficult to confirm in photos.

Why is there no fixed shipping price?

Destination, route, actual and volumetric weight, packaging and item type all affect the final price.
