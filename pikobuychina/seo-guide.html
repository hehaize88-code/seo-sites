Pikobuy Spreadsheet 2026 Guide: W2C Finds, QC Photos and Shipping Workflow

This complete guide explains how buyers organize product research, evaluate W2C sources, review QC photos and prepare international shipping decisions.

Introduction to Pikobuy Spreadsheet

A Pikobuy Spreadsheet is a structured shopping research method. Instead of saving disconnected links, buyers can organize products, categories, source information, QC notes and shipping plans in one place.

The purpose of a spreadsheet is not only finding products. It creates a repeatable workflow from discovery to final parcel preparation.

Understanding W2C Finds

W2C means where to find a product. A useful W2C entry should include product information, images, category, size notes and source details.

Before purchasing, buyers should review current product information and compare different options. Clear research helps avoid mistakes caused by incomplete links.

QC Photos: What Buyers Should Check

QC photos provide a visual inspection opportunity before shipping. Buyers can review overall shape, color accuracy, stitching, materials, labels, accessories and packaging.

For shoes, check symmetry and construction. For clothing, review measurements, tags and fabric appearance. For accessories, inspect hardware and finishing details.

Shipping Workflow and Cost Planning

International shipping costs are affected by destination, parcel weight, volume, packaging and route selection.

Good planning means checking product details first, approving QC information, then choosing a suitable shipping method.

Building a Better Spreadsheet

A practical spreadsheet can include product name, W2C link, category, price estimate, QC notes, size information, shipping risk and final decision.

This allows buyers to compare products over time and maintain useful purchase history.

Advanced Research Tips

Experienced buyers record previous experiences, compare products, save useful sources and improve future decisions through organized notes.

A strong spreadsheet turns shopping research into a long-term system rather than a temporary list.
