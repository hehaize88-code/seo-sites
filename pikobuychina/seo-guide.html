W2C

How to use W2C routes without losing context

A useful W2C page should answer more than “where is the item?” It should show the product category, route source, visible product details, expected risk points and what the buyer should check before moving forward. PikobuyChina organizes product intent into clearer paths so visitors can compare shoes, apparel, accessories and electronics before opening a final product page.

For every product route, keep a note of the category, product name, visible image, price estimate, size notes and any warning that could affect the parcel. When a route contains only a title and no supporting details, treat it as unfinished research rather than a final purchase decision.

QC Photos

QC photo checklist before parcel approval

Shape and proportion. Compare the overall silhouette, left-right symmetry and visible construction.
Material and texture. Look for mismatched fabric, unexpected shine, thin panels, rough surfaces or color differences.
Labels and details. Review tags, print placement, stitching, hardware, zippers and size labels.
Packaging and accessories. Confirm whether boxes, dust bags, extra laces or small parts are included when they matter to the item.

Weight

Heavier items, shoes with boxes and layered clothing may raise international cost. Keep estimated weight in the product note before building a haul.

Volume

Large boxes and bulky items can create volume-weight concerns. Combine products only after checking size and packaging expectations.

Restrictions

Electronics, liquids, batteries and branded packaging may affect route choices. Verify current platform and carrier rules before shipment.

Spreadsheet

A simple spreadsheet method for PikobuyChina visitors

Create columns for product name, category, source route, price estimate, QC notes, size notes, shipping risk and final decision. Do not approve a product only because it appears in a list. A spreadsheet works best when it supports inspection, not when it replaces inspection.
